FASHION ATLAS

PRE-MODERN / ANTIQUITY

Core systems of drape, civic dress, and early tailoring

Ancient Egypt (c. 3000 BCE – 300 BCE)

Silhouettes & Garments: Kalasiris sheath dresses for women; pleated linen kilts (shendyt) for men; wrapped and belted styles as signifiers of class.

Materials & Textiles: Fine, sheer linen (status indicated by fineness and whiteness); gold thread for elite use.

Accessories & Symbolism: Wigs, broad beaded collars (wesekh), kohl-rimmed eyes, symbolic crowns (Atef, Nemes).

Historiography: Dress tied to religious and cosmic order (ma’at); nudity coded as innocence vs. luxury.

Legacy Echoes: Sheer white columnar dresses (20th c. couture); Art Deco revival of Egyptian motifs post-Tutankhamun discovery (1920s).

Ancient Greece (c. 1200 BCE – 146 BCE)

Silhouettes & Garments: Draped chitons (wool or linen), peplos, himation cloaks; modular and gender-fluid in cut.

Materials & Textiles: Wool dominant; linen, later imported silk for elites.

Accessories & Symbolism: Laurel wreaths, belts to alter silhouette; sandals with straps up the calf.

Historiography: Classical ideal of proportion → Renaissance through Neoclassicism.

Legacy Echoes: Bias-cut gowns (Vionnet); Olympic ceremonial dress; minimalist drape aesthetics in modernism.

MEDIEVAL & EARLY MODERN EUROPE

From ecclesiastical codes to secular court display; tailoring develops

Early Medieval (c. 500–1000)

Silhouettes & Garments: Tunics, mantles, leggings; layered practical dress influenced by climate.

Materials & Textiles: Wool and linen widespread; silks as imports through Byzantium.

Accessories & Symbolism: Brooches, clasps, and jewelry marking tribal affiliation and rank.

Historiography: Often termed “Dark Ages” (outdated); instead, rich regional textile cultures.

Legacy Echoes: Revivalist medievalism in Gothic and Pre-Raphaelite fashion imagery.
