1. Historical Fashion Report (Click to Expand/Collapse)

Ancient Greece (c. 800 BCE – 400 CE)

Historiography: Clothing reflected social hierarchy, climate, and functional needs. Draped garments allowed mobility while conveying status; the aesthetic emphasized natural body lines. The era faded as Rome’s imperial influence introduced tailored tunics and more complex layering.

Prevailing Styles & Silhouettes: Chiton, himation, peplos; simple, draped forms emphasizing natural body lines.

Materials: Linen, wool, sometimes silk for elite garments.

Signifiers & Accessories: Leather sandals, bronze/gold jewelry, belts for waist definition.

Ancient Rome (c. 500 BCE – 400 CE)

Historiography: Roman fashion reflected citizenship and social order, responding to Greek influence but formalized with togas and tunics. The fall of Rome led to more regionalized, simplified medieval dress.

Prevailing Styles & Silhouettes: Tunics for men and women, toga draping for status, stola for women.

Materials: Wool, linen, silk for elites, dyed fabrics.

Signifiers & Accessories: Brooches, belts, sandals, jewelry denoting rank.

View Images

Early Medieval Europe (c. 500–1000)

Historiography: After Rome, simpler garments reflected rural, decentralized life; practicality dominated due to climate and social instability.

Prevailing Styles & Silhouettes: Tunics, simple dresses, cloaks for warmth.

Materials: Wool, linen, fur trims.

Signifiers & Accessories: Brooches, belts, leather shoes.
